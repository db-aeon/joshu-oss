Joshu is an AI assistant that works less like a chatbot and more like a colleague at the next desk — one you reach by text, call, email, or screen-share, including real-time voice over the phone and in your browser. Instead of living in a chat window, each Joshu has its own dedicated cloud computer: an AI-native desktop, available from any web browser with no machine to run or maintain, where files are both visible to you and semantically indexed so the AI truly understands them. Joshu operates its own suite of apps (mail, calendar, docs, sheets, and more) and includes an embedded stealth browser — log into any site once, and your Joshu takes it from there, unsupervised, with no per-site integration required. Two proprietary systems make the delegation real: a human-modeled memory architecture that remembers the people, context, and standards behind your work so you're never briefing a stranger, and a workflow engine modeled on how a great chief of staff actually plans and executes multi-step tasks. The result is an assistant that keeps working while you don't, gets to know you over time, and is built for the small-business owners and solo operators who are their own marketing, ops, and admin team — for a flat monthly fee, a fraction of the cost of human help.
