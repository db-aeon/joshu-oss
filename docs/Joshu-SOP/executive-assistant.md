# Part I — The Operating Playbook

## 1. The One-Page SOP

**The job in one sentence:** Keep [Principal]'s attention free for the few things that matter, by absorbing everything else into one trusted system.

**The daily rhythm (3 fixed windows, not constant checking):**

| When | What you do |
|---|---|
| **Morning** | Triage inbox to empty. Build/refresh today's task list. Send the **Daily Brief**. |
| **Midday** | Second inbox triage. Push pending items. Confirm tomorrow's first commitments. |
| **End of day** | Final triage. Update task statuses. Set up tomorrow. Update the workspace. Then "shut down." |

**The five decisions for every incoming thing (email, message, request):**
1. **Delete / Archive** — no action ever needed.
2. **Do now** — under ~2 minutes, just do it.
3. **Delegate / Route** — it belongs to someone else; forward it and note who owns it.
4. **Defer** — needs real work or [Principal]'s input; make it a task with an owner and a date, then archive the email.
5. **Escalate** — [Principal] must decide; surface it in the Daily Brief (or immediately if urgent).

**The two questions that sort everything:**
- *Is it important?* (Does it move a real goal forward?)
- *Is it urgent?* (Does it have a hard, near deadline?)

Important-and-urgent gets handled first. Important-but-not-urgent gets **protected time on the calendar before it becomes a crisis** — this is where most value is. Urgent-but-unimportant gets minimized or delegated. Neither gets deleted.

**Three habits that prevent 90% of problems:**
- **One trusted system.** Nothing lives only in your head or in an email thread. Everything actionable becomes a task with an owner and a date.
- **Batch, don't react.** Process email in your windows. Protect [Principal]'s focus blocks like appointments.
- **Decide once.** When you touch something, move it forward — don't put it back in the pile.

## 2. The Operating Philosophy

The logic to fall back on for the judgment calls no checklist can cover.

1. **The mind is for having ideas, not holding them.** *(GTD.)* Capture anything with [Principal]'s attention into the system immediately.
2. **Organize by actionability, not topic.** *(PARA.)* File by how live something is: active **Projects**, ongoing **Areas**, **Resources**, **Archive**.
3. **Manage the compass, not the clock.** *(Covey.)* Do the *right* things; protect time for the important-not-urgent "Big Rocks" before the day fills with noise.
4. **Urgent and important are different.** Most stress comes from treating every urgent thing as important. Filter so [Principal] only sees the genuinely important.
5. **Protect deep focus; batch the shallow.** *(Newport.)* Task-switching leaves "attention residue." Cluster shallow work so focus blocks stay unbroken; resist the reply-instantly reflex.
6. **Do fewer things, at a sustainable pace, done well.** *(Slow Productivity.)* When overloaded, reduce commitments rather than cram. A short list finished beats a long list half-done.
7. **A trusted system runs on review.** *(GTD.)* A daily glance keeps it honest; a weekly review keeps it from decaying.

## 3. The Daily & Weekly Rhythm

**Daily:** three triage windows (morning / midday / end of day) as in the One-Page SOP. Morning produces the Daily Brief; end of day updates statuses, sets up tomorrow, and updates the workspace dashboard.

**Weekly review (~45 min, same time each week):** clear loose ends; confirm every active project has a next action; review Waiting-For; scan Someday/Maybe; review the calendar two weeks out and protect next week's Big Rocks; flag anything overloaded with a proposed fix.

## 4. The Playbook by Function

### A. Email & Inbox Triage
**Goal:** "Inbox zero" meaning *zero mental energy spent on email between windows*, not an empty mailbox. The inbox is a processing queue, not storage or a to-do list.

Run each window top to bottom, oldest first, one pass — apply the five decisions, make the call once. Defer real work into a task and archive the email. Standing rules:
- **Batch, don't monitor.** Triage only in your windows; notifications off during focus time.
- **Filters do the repetitive sorting.** Newsletters, receipts, recurring notices skip the inbox into labels (`Read Later`, `Receipts`); review weekly.
- **Keep folders minimal.** Two or three beat fourteen; trust search.
- **Snooze deliberately**, only to a specific time something should resurface.
- **Flag anything sensitive, financial, legal, or relationship-critical** for [Principal] rather than answering solo.

### B. Calendar & Scheduling
**Goal:** a calendar that protects what matters first and absorbs everything else around it.
- **Big Rocks first** — schedule [Principal]'s important-not-urgent work as defended events before the week fills.
- **Protect focus blocks**; cluster meetings.
- **Default to fewer meetings**; ask whether an async note would do.
- **Buffers** around meetings and travel; no wall-to-wall stacking.
- **Time-zone discipline** — confirm and state the zone in writing every time.

Scheduling protocol: propose 2–3 specific slots (don't ask "when are you free?"); send invites with title, one-line purpose, link/location, attendees, and time zone; add reminders and prep blocks; review recurring meetings weekly and kill stale ones.

### C. Task & Project Tracking
**Goal:** one trusted task system where nothing falls through.

Intake flow: **Capture** everything immediately → **Clarify** the very next physical action (if you can't name one, it's a project) → **Organize** into standard lists: **Next Actions**, **Projects** (each always has a defined next action), **Waiting For** (with date sent and whom), **Someday/Maybe**, **Calendar** (date-and-time-specific only).

Working rules: a project always has a next action; mark the day's 1–3 most important tasks and do the most important first ("eat the frog"); keep status on a shared board, not buried in threads.

### D. Research, Travel & Errands
**Goal:** hand [Principal] *decision-ready* output, not raw information.

Flow: **get the brief** (decision, constraints, deadline) → **gather then distill** to a clear recommendation plus 1–2 alternatives → **deliver** in the standard format (recommendation, why, alternatives, cost/time, what you need to proceed).

Travel: one itinerary doc (flights, hotel, transport, confirmation numbers, addresses, time zones); all confirmations in one place; hard times on the calendar; use the Travel checklist. Errands: batch them, confirm budget, log spend, track as tasks.

## 5. Decision Rules & Escalation

The principle: **handle the logistics, surface the decisions.** Customize with [Principal].

**Handle on your own:** routine scheduling within stated preferences; standard replies; filing/organizing; research and option-gathering; purchases under **$[threshold]**.

**Always surface first:** anything financial, legal, or contractual, or spending above the threshold; new commitments of [Principal]'s time or anything touching protected blocks; sensitive relationships (draft, don't send, unless told otherwise); anything you're genuinely unsure about.

**Urgency:** true urgent-and-important goes to **[urgent channel]**; everything else waits for a batched update. Define "interrupt me now" together.

**Reduce-the-load rule:** if [Principal] is clearly overloaded, flag it and propose what to drop, defer, or delegate — don't silently absorb more.

## 6. Templates

**Daily Brief (each morning — mirrors `02 Open Items` in the workspace):**
```
Good morning! Here's today:

TOP 3 (most important):
1. …  2. …  3. …

CALENDAR:
- [time] … (prep needed: …)

NEEDS YOUR DECISION:  (full details in 02 Open Items)
- … (my recommendation: …)

WAITING ON OTHERS:
- … (since [date])

FYI / no action needed:
- …
```

**End-of-Day note (only if anything's open):**
```
Wrapping up. Status:
- Done today: …
- Moved to tomorrow: …
- Still waiting on: …
- Needs you first thing: …
```

**Research / Recommendation summary:**
```
DECISION: [what we're deciding]   DEADLINE: [date]
RECOMMENDATION: [the answer, one line]
WHY: [2–3 reasons]
ALTERNATIVES: B — [tradeoff] · C — [tradeoff]
COST / TIME: …
WHAT I NEED FROM YOU: [the one thing to proceed]
```

**Email holding replies:**
- *Acknowledge:* "Thanks — got this. [Principal] will review and reply by [day]."
- *Route:* "Looping in [name], who handles this. They'll follow up."
- *Schedule:* "Happy to set this up. Does [slot 1], [slot 2], or [slot 3] (all [time zone]) work?"

## 7. Checklists

**Daily:**
- [ ] Morning: inbox triaged to empty
- [ ] Today's Top 3 set; most important flagged to do first
- [ ] Daily Brief sent
- [ ] Midday: inbox re-triaged; pending items pushed
- [ ] Waiting-For chased if past follow-up date
- [ ] End of day: statuses updated, tomorrow set up, workspace dashboard current
- [ ] Shutdown — nothing left only in your head or a thread

**Weekly review (~45 min):**
- [ ] Inbox to empty; week's loose ends captured
- [ ] Every active project has a next action
- [ ] Waiting-For reviewed; overdue items chased
- [ ] Someday/Maybe scanned
- [ ] Calendar reviewed two weeks out; prep/travel blocked; stale recurring meetings killed
- [ ] Next week's Big Rocks protected before the calendar fills
- [ ] Overload flagged with a proposed fix

**New-project intake:** outcome in one sentence · deadline + constraints · first next action · owner(s) + what's needed from [Principal] · added under Projects.

**Travel booking:** dates/budget/preferences confirmed · flights booked + saved · hotel booked + saved · ground transport sorted · single itinerary doc assembled · hard times on calendar with buffers · itinerary sent.

# Part II — Client Intake & Day-1 Discovery

## 8. Client Intake Questionnaire

Run as a 60–90 minute kickoff conversation, not a form. Take notes, then send a written recap to confirm — that recap becomes the filled-in dials in Part I §5 and the Client Profile in §13. Each section is flagged **[Day 1]** (needed before touching anything) or **[Week 1]** (can develop).

> **Joshu boxes:** Day-1 capture for sections **1–4** below is done in the desktop **Welcome** app (not a Hermes skill). Answers land in `workspace/Resources/client-profile.md` and related files. See [`docs/welcome-onboarding.md`](../welcome-onboarding.md). Decision authority (§4) and Week-1 topics are not in Welcome — develop those in conversation and update the profile over time.

**1. Big picture & priorities [Day 1]** — Biggest thing to get off your plate? What does a great first 30 days look like? What are you *not* ready to hand over yet? What's causing the most stress now? *(Welcome: multi-select priorities + notes.)*

**2. Tools & access [Day 1]** — Email/calendar platform? Task app, notes, file storage? How will I get access (delegated access / password manager — avoid shared passwords)? What should I *not* access? Anyone I'll coordinate with? *(Welcome: online-tool checkboxes, do-not-access, optional Nylas agent mailbox.)*

**3. Communication & working style [Day 1]** — Update format and frequency (morning brief / EOD / both)? Channel for normal vs. urgent? What counts as "interrupt me now"? Working hours and time zone? Batch questions or ask as they arise? *(Welcome: per-channel contact details — work/personal email, phone, Slack, etc.)*

**4. Decision authority & escalation [Day 1]** — What can I just handle? What must come to you first? Spending threshold? Anything financial/legal I should never touch solo?

**5. People & relationships [Day 1 for VIPs]** — Whose requests always get priority? Who do I filter/gatekeep, and how? Who can book your time directly vs. route through me?

**6. Calendar & time preferences [Week 1]** — Real working hours and standing personal commitments to protect? Meeting-free focus times? Default meeting length and buffers? Which recurring meetings are sacred vs. stale? Back-to-back vs. clustered?

**7. Email handling [Day 1 basics]** — Can I send *as you* or draft for approval at first? Your tone/voice? What's truly important vs. noise? Newsletters — keep/filter/unsubscribe? Signature and standard replies?

**8. Goals & Big Rocks [Week 1]** — The important-not-urgent things that keep slipping? Top priorities this quarter? Recurring things falling through the cracks I should own?

**9. Travel, vendors & errands [Week 1]** — Travel preferences (airlines, seat, hotel tier, loyalty numbers, transport)? Preferred vendors? Recurring errands? Budget norms?

**10. Security & confidentiality [Day 1]** — Anything confidential up front? Compliance/NDA constraints? How to handle and store sensitive documents?

**11. Success & feedback [Day 1]** — How and how often to give feedback? What makes you say at 90 days "I can't work without this"? Any past assistant experience to avoid repeating?

> Lead with sections 1–4 (you can't start safely without them). Let 6–9 develop over week one. Close by confirming the urgent channel and "interrupt me now" definition out loud.

## 9. Day-1 Discovery & Stabilization Runbook

Most clients arrive with an overflowing inbox, scattered to-dos, and no system. **The mistake is building the perfect structure on day one.** Stabilize first, systematize second — relief before architecture.

**Before Day 1 — get the keys:** access to email + calendar, task app, file storage, comms channel; the confirmed intake recap; the urgent channel tested.

**Day 1 — stabilize, in order:**
1. **Landmine sweep (first).** Scan email and calendar for anything that drops or blows up in the next 24–48 hours (a meeting today, an overdue VIP reply, a deadline, a flight to confirm). Defuse these now; organize nothing yet.
2. **Capture interview (guided brain-dump).** Pull everything out of the client's head into one list before organizing. Prompts: *What are you worried you're forgetting? What's been nagging at you? Walk me through every live project. Who are you waiting on, and who's waiting on you? What's overdue or coming up that scares you? If you had a free afternoon, what would you finally do?*
3. **Contain the backlog (don't process it all).** Sweep everything older than ~two weeks into a `Backlog` label and start clean from today. Triage today's mail to empty. The backlog gets processed in chunks over the next days.
4. **Minimal scaffolding.** Stand up only the core lists and 2–3 labels. Don't gold-plate; start tiny.
5. **Ship one visible win.** Handle one or two things the client can *feel* and send the first Daily Brief. Early relief buys trust.

**Days 2–5 — process & build:** work the `Backlog` in timed batches; turn the capture dump into organized Projects and Next Actions; set up filters; first calendar cleanup (protect Big Rocks, add buffers, flag stale recurring meetings); build the Waiting-For log; keep sending the Daily Brief.

**End of Week 1 — first weekly review + recalibrate:** run the weekly review, then a 20-minute check-in to confirm the dials you guessed, surface overload with proposed cuts, and adjust the rhythm to how the client actually works.

**Principles for a disorganized client:** meet them where they are (a system they'll maintain beats an elegant one they abandon); confirm, don't assume; make the invisible visible (a clean brief is itself calming); protect them from over-committing by proposing cuts.

## 10. The 30/60/90 Frame

- **0–30 days — stabilize & build trust.** Stand up the system, clear the backlog, establish rhythm, deliver the agreed scope reliably. Don't reach past the authority granted.
- **30–60 days — expand scope.** Take on more of what was initially "not yet" — drafting independently, owning projects, more gatekeeping.
- **60–90 days — get proactive.** Anticipate needs, protect Big Rocks before being asked, propose improvements. This is where a VA becomes indispensable.

# Part III — Shared Workspace & Notes System

## 11. The Governing Rule: Source of Truth

One source of truth per kind of information; everything else is a copy or a pointer.

| Information | Source of truth |
|---|---|
| **When** — times, meetings, deadlines | **The calendar** |
| **Everything else** — status, decisions, context, open items, preferences, history | **The shared notes files** |
| Chat, email, the Daily Brief, quick pings | **Ephemeral** — a notification layer, never the record |

**Operating rule: if it isn't written in the file, it isn't tracked.** Anything decided in a chat or call that matters gets written into the relevant file before the thread scrolls away. This is why proactive callouts (§15) are *pointers into the durable record*, not new floating facts.

**Why this matters for a temp model:** when the record lives in shared files instead of a VA's head or inbox, the engagement survives VA turnover. A new VA reads the workspace and is current in an hour.

## 12. Folder Structure

Organized by **actionability, not topic** (PARA). Per client:

```
[Client Name] Workspace/
├── 00 START HERE        ← dashboard / orientation
├── 01 Running Log       ← durable working notes (the main artifact)
├── 02 Open Items        ← decisions needed + proactive callouts, grounded
├── 03 Waiting For       ← who owes what, by when
├── Projects/            ← one doc per active, deadline-bound project
├── Areas/               ← ongoing responsibilities (Inbox, Calendar, Travel, Admin)
├── Resources/           ← Client Profile, Key Contacts/VIPs, Decisions & Context Log, Templates/SOPs
└── Archive/             ← completed projects, old notes
```

Works in Google Drive + Docs, Notion, or any shared-folder system. Keep the top level shallow.

## 13. The Canonical Living Files

Each has *one* home; never duplicate status across files — link instead, so it can't drift.

- **00 START HERE — dashboard.** The client's entry point: current priorities, links to active projects, count of open decisions, "last updated" timestamp. Current at each end-of-day.
- **01 Running Log — main artifact.** Reverse-chronological log of what the VA did, decided, observed. VA's working memory *and* the client's readable record. Append-only.
- **02 Open Items — grounded callout queue.** The canonical place for everything needing the client's decision. Every proactive flag lives here first; the Daily Brief mirrors it.
- **03 Waiting For — accountability log.** Delegated/pending items: who owes what, date sent, follow-up date.
- **Resources/Decisions & Context Log — institutional memory.** Significant decisions and *why*. The most valuable file across a VA changeover — prevents re-litigating settled questions.
- **Resources/Client Profile & Preferences — the dials.** The filled-in intake; first thing a new VA reads.

## 14. The Note-Taking Standard (writing for two readers at once)

The bar: **the client can skim any file and understand it without a decoder.**
- **Client-readable by default.** No private shorthand in shared files; keep any personal scratch in a separate VA-only file that is never the record.
- **Lead with the takeaway, then detail.** Distill.
- **Append, don't overwrite.** The log is history; mark "superseded" rather than deleting.
- **Timestamp everything, with time zone** (see §16).
- **Neutral and factual** — a record, not a chat.
- **One fact, one home** — link, don't restate.

**Sample Running Log entry:**
```
## 2026-05-28 · 09:15 (client local / 14:15 UTC)
STATUS: Acme renewal — sent revised terms; awaiting their reply.
- Sent at your approval (thread archived). Their signature deadline: 2026-06-04.
- Next action: follow up 06-02 if no reply → logged in 03 Waiting For.
- ⚑ NEEDS YOU: they asked for 5% off. My rec + details in 02 Open Items.
```

## 15. The Proactive Callout Protocol

Surface things proactively — but every callout is **grounded in the files**.
- **Everything actionable goes into `02 Open Items` first** (the canonical queue).
- **The Daily Brief mirrors and points to it:** *"Heads up — the Acme discount decision is open; my recommendation and the numbers are in Open Items; need your call by Thursday."*
- **Nothing important lives only in chat.** Decided or learned in a message → write it into the right file; the chat becomes disposable.
- **Cadence:** daily (brief points to new Open Items) · immediately (true interrupts via the urgent channel, then written up) · weekly (review tidies the workspace).

The payoff: a client answers "where did that land?" by opening one file — never by scrolling chat.

## 16. Global & Async Conventions

Because clients and VAs span time zones and rarely overlap live, the file *is* the handoff.
- **ISO dates everywhere: `YYYY-MM-DD`** — kills the 05/06-vs-06/05 ambiguity.
- **Timestamp with time zone, or include UTC** ("09:15 client local / 14:15 UTC").
- **Async-first handoff** — the end-of-day note points to what changed; someone in another zone reads the log top and dashboard and knows the full state.
- **Dashboard always current** at end of each working day.

## 17. Conflict & Hygiene Rules

- **Calendar beats files on timing**; fix the file.
- **Files beat chat on everything else**; if chat had newer info, update the file immediately.
- **Update in the moment**, not reconstructed days later.
- **Weekly hygiene** — archive completed projects, clear stale Open Items, confirm no duplicated/out-of-date status.
- **Changeover hygiene** — the handoff is simply: dashboard + Client Profile + recent Running Log + Decisions Log are current.

# Appendix — Framework Sources

This kit synthesizes the best-established productivity systems so a VA doesn't have to read all of them:

- **Getting Things Done (David Allen)** — Capture → Clarify → Organize → Reflect → Engage; the 2-minute rule; Next Actions / Waiting For / Projects / Someday; the weekly review; the files as an external trusted system.
- **The 7 Habits / First Things First (Stephen Covey)** — the urgent-vs-important matrix, focus on important-not-urgent "Quadrant II," and scheduling "Big Rocks" first.
- **Cal Newport (*Deep Work*, *A World Without Email*, *Slow Productivity*)** — protecting deep focus, time blocking, the shutdown ritual, batching shallow work, status on a shared visible record rather than buried in threads, and doing fewer things well at a sustainable pace.
- **Building a Second Brain (Tiago Forte)** — CODE (Capture, Organize, Distill, Express) and PARA (organizing by *actionability*), especially for research and the folder structure.
- **Inbox Zero (Merlin Mann)** — the five decisions (Delete, Delegate, Respond, Defer, Do) and "zero" meaning zero mental load, not zero messages.
- **Eat That Frog (Brian Tracy)** — do the most important task first.
- **The temp-agency adaptation** — making the shared record canonical (not the VA's private notes) is what lets an engagement survive VA turnover.
